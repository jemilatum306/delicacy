Ghanaian Dining Traditions

Meals in Ghana are more than just food - they're social events that bring families and communities together. Many traditional meals are eaten communally from the same bowl, using the right hand.

Breaking bread together (or more accurately, banku together) is a sign of friendship and hospitality in Ghanaian culture.

Street food is an integral part of daily life, with vendors selling everything from grilled plantains to spicy kebabs throughout cities.
